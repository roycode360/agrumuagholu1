Peace Returns To Assemblies Of God Church, Sapele

Peace and tranquillity has finally returned to the Assemblies of God Church, Sapele, in Sapele local government Area of Delta State following the affirmation of Rev. (Dr.) R. O. Itotoh as substantive District Superintendent of the church in the area by members and the subsequent appointment and confirmation of Rev Napoleon Abada as local pastor of the controversial branch two, at Atufe road in Sapele metropolis. Regular Sunday Church service that had hitherto eluded the worshipers at the Church for the past two months as a result of the leadership tussle which culminated in crisis that led to many being injured and consequently police case, took place at the branch two last Sunday with relief and jubilation.

Addressing the faithful worshipers who had long been denied of solemn congregation and the Assembly of God’s people as enjoined by the bible, the district superintendent, Rev Itotoh in all humility and meekness of the spirit, apologized fervently to the congregation for all the troubles and embarrassment the tussle may have caused them. Rev Itotoh acknowledged the fact that ‘’members spiritual life may have waned, affected and bruised during the trials and turbulent times attributed it to the work of the devil who crept in subtly to destroy the good work’’ adding however, that ‘’it has worked out for the furtherance of the gospel to the glory of God’’. He, therefore, admonished members to remain fervent and resolute while they continue to strive and earnestly contend for the faith which was once delivered unto the saints.

In his acceptance speech, Rev. Napoleon Abada declared his readiness to lift the banner of truth and righteousness through the preaching of the gospel and called for members support in prayers so as to move the church forward. It will be recalled that at the height of the leadership crisis that engulfed the Assemblies of God Church, Sapele district, the State government, through the office of Secretary to the state governor, Comrade Ovuzoure Macauley intervened and admonished the leadership of the church to ensure that they live up to their calling of taking care of the flocks over which the Holy Ghost had made them overseers.
